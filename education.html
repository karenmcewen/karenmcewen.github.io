Royal E. Cabell Fellowship at Northwestern University

For my senior independent study thesis, we had the opportunity to research one of the standing projects, or to create one of our own. I chose to do a new project, on the topic of self-organization of physical systems. Anyone who has played at the beach knows that if you pour sand into a pile, it will build up until it reaches a certain angle (known as a critical angle) at which point avalanches start to form. At the critical angle, the avalanches are of all sizes.

With the help of my advisors, Dr. John Lindner and Dr. Donald Jacobs, I sought to research this phenomena through physical experiment, mathematical theory, and computer modeling. The process was so successful, that I received a grant to continue the research during the summer, and the project became one of the primary research areas of the department. Students have worked on it every year for 27 years!
